About Thomas Alva Edison

Thomas Alva Edison (February 11, 1847 – October 18, 1931) was an American inventor and businessman who has been described as America's greatest inventor. He developed many devices in fields such as electric power generation, mass communication, sound recording, and motion pictures. These inventions, which include the phonograph, the motion picture camera, and the long-lasting, practical electric light bulb, have had a widespread impact on the modern industrialized world.

Timeline of Thomas Edison's Life

  • 1847: Born in Milan, Ohio, USA.
  • 1869: Receives his first patent for the electric vote recorder.
  • 1877: Invents the phonograph.
  • 1879: Develops a practical electric light bulb.
  • 1880: Establishes Edison Illuminating Company.
  • 1887: Opens the first industrial research lab in Menlo Park, New Jersey.
  • 1891: Patents the Kinetoscope, an early motion picture device.
  • 1912: Introduces the Edison storage battery.
  • 1931: Passes away in West Orange, New Jersey, USA.

Legacy

Edison was a prolific inventor, holding 1,093 US patents in his name, as well as many patents in the United Kingdom, France, and Germany. His work and inventions greatly influenced modern life, leading to major innovations in electric power, mass communication, and other fields.
